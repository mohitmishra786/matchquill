# MatchQuill vs. Teal

> An honest comparison. Teal is a strong, well-funded all-in-one job-search platform; we concede
> its strengths and position MatchQuill only where it genuinely differs. Every competitive claim
> is sourced — see **## Sources**. Pricing varies across sources; verify on tealhq.com.

---

## What Teal is genuinely good at (concede first)

Teal is one of the most polished job-search platforms available, and it is stronger than
MatchQuill on several fronts:

- **Best-in-class job tracking (CRM-style).** Teal is *tracker-first* — a dashboard for saving
  roles, tracking application status, and managing companies and contacts. This is its core
  strength and it is genuinely good.
- **A Chrome extension that saves jobs from 40+ boards** directly into the tracker, auto-detecting
  compensation. (So: Teal has a browser extension too — we will not pretend it doesn't.)
- **A full AI writing suite and ATS tooling** — bullet generator, summary writer, resume rewriter,
  cover-letter tool, JD-match scoring, and an ATS readability scanner.
- **Scale and credibility** — a large user base, venture backing, and strong Chrome Web Store and
  Trustpilot ratings (verify current figures at source).

If you want a structured system to *manage an entire job search pipeline*, Teal is a category
leader and MatchQuill does not try to replace that.

## Where MatchQuill is different

MatchQuill is not a job-search CRM. It is narrower and deeper on one thing: turning a specific
posting into a tailored, truthful document.

| | Teal | MatchQuill |
|---|---|---|
| Center of gravity | Track & organize the whole search (CRM) | Compile one tailored resume + cover letter per posting |
| Browser extension purpose | Save jobs into the tracker | Extract the JD locally to drive compilation |
| Generation guardrail | AI writing suite (general-purpose) | Zero-hallucination constraint — grounded in your validated profile, invents nothing |
| Matching | Keyword matching + skills extraction, JD-match scoring | Keyword relevance + **optional** semantic-embedding layer (off by default, see caveat) |
| Pipeline/tracking | **Yes — a core strength** | Not a feature we offer |

**The honest summary:** Teal is the better *pipeline manager*, and both products ship a browser
extension. The difference is intent: Teal's extension feeds a **tracker**; MatchQuill's extension
feeds a **compiler** whose defining constraint is that it will not fabricate your history. If your
bottleneck is organizing 50 applications, use Teal. If your bottleneck is producing a resume that
is both tailored to *this* posting and provably grounded in your real experience, that is what
MatchQuill is built for. They can coexist.

> On semantic matching: MatchQuill's optional semantic layer uses sentence embeddings to
> recognize experience phrased differently than the posting but meaning the same thing. It is
> **off by default**, feature-flagged, requires an optional install, and its production
> performance is unverified. We present it as a capability, not a default-on advantage.
