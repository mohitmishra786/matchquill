# MatchQuill vs. Jobscan

> An honest comparison. Jobscan is the category-defining ATS scanner; we concede its strengths
> plainly and position MatchQuill only where it genuinely differs. Every competitive claim is
> sourced — see **## Sources**. Pricing changes often; verify on jobscan.co.

---

## What Jobscan is genuinely good at (concede first)

Jobscan is the most mature keyword-match analyzer in the category, and we are not going to
pretend otherwise:

- **A refined, well-calibrated match-rate score.** Paste your resume and a job description and
  Jobscan returns a 0–100% match rate benchmarked against real ATS behavior, with a recommended
  target (~75%). Years of iteration have made this the most detailed scanner you can buy.
- **ATS-platform detection.** Jobscan identifies which specific ATS a posting uses (Workday,
  Greenhouse, Taleo, iCIMS, etc.) and tailors its recommendations to that platform's parsing
  quirks. This is a real, differentiated feature.
- **Skills-gap analysis + formatting/searchability checks** — separate hard-skill and soft-skill
  breakdowns, plus flags for tables, images, and layouts that ATS software cannot parse.
- **A large body of guidance and a big user base** built up over years.

If your goal is to *diagnose* a resume against a posting and learn exactly which keywords you are
missing, Jobscan is excellent at that job.

## Where Jobscan stops (verified)

Reviewers consistently note one boundary: **Jobscan diagnoses; it does not write.** Its core
scanner tells you what is missing and leaves the rewriting to you. Its premium "AI Optimize"
feature can rewrite bullets, but the product's center of gravity is analysis, and its scanner
workflow is **paste-based** — you copy the resume and the job description into the tool. (Sources
below.)

## Where MatchQuill is different

| | Jobscan | MatchQuill |
|---|---|---|
| Core job | Score/diagnose a resume vs. a JD | Compile a tailored resume + cover letter from your profile |
| Job description input | Paste into the scanner | Extension extracts it locally from the page (LinkedIn, Indeed, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, iCIMS, and more) |
| Content generation | Analyzer first; AI rewrite as a premium add-on | Generation is the product, constrained to your validated profile |
| Fabrication risk | You control edits | Zero-hallucination constraint: rephrases/reorders/highlights, does not invent history |
| Matching | Keyword match with contextual variations | Keyword relevance + **optional** semantic-embedding layer (off by default, see caveat) |
| ATS-platform detection | **Yes — mature** | Not a feature we claim |

**The honest summary:** Jobscan is the better *scanner* and has ATS-platform detection MatchQuill
does not offer. MatchQuill is not trying to be a better scanner — it is a **compiler**. It reads
the posting off the page and produces a tailored, ATS-ready document grounded in what you have
actually done, rather than handing you a score and a to-do list.

> On semantic matching: MatchQuill's optional semantic layer uses sentence embeddings to
> recognize experience phrased differently than the posting but meaning the same thing. It is
> **off by default**, feature-flagged, requires an optional install, and its production
> performance is unverified. We present it as a capability, not a default-on advantage — and note
> that Jobscan's 2026 scanner also claims to understand contextual variations (e.g. treating
> "project management" and "managed projects" similarly), so semantic understanding is not unique
> to us.
